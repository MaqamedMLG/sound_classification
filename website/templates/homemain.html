HOW IT WORKS?

The system utilizes the ESC-50 dataset, a comprehensive collection of 2,000 environmental sound recordings, to accurately classify different types of sounds. Each sound clip in the dataset is five seconds long and meticulously categorized into 50 classes across five major categories — ranging from natural soundscapes to human noises and urban sounds.

It learns to identify intricate patterns and characteristics specific to each sound class through a series of convolutional layers. This approach enables the model to understand and distinguish between the fine details of different environmental sounds, from the gentle sprinkle of rain to the distinctive singing of birds.
